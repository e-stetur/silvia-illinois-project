Tourist Attractions

Millennium Park

Millennium Park opened in 16 july 2004 and quickly became Chicago's most iconic destination. Besides Cloud Gate, the park features Crown Fountain with interactive water displays, the Jay Pritzker Pavilion for free concerts, and the Lurie Garden. It's the perfect spot for picnics, photography, and experiencing Chicago's vibrant culture.

Location: Chicago

Hours: 6 AM - 11 PM daily

Addmision: Free

Navy Pier

Navy Pier opened in 1916 and has become Chicago's most visited attraction. Besides the iconic 200-foot Centennial Wheel, the pier features the Chicago Children's Museum, IMAX theater, boat tours, and waterfront restaurants. It's the perfect spot for entertainment, lakefront views, fireworks displays, and experiencing Chicago's vibrant atmosphere.

Popular tourist destination with dining and entertainment

Location: Chicago

Hours: 10 AM - 10 PM

Admission: Free entry

Places to Dine

Giordano's

Giordano's has been serving Chicago's famous stuffed deep-dish pizza since 1974. Each pizza takes about 45 minutes to bake and easily feeds 3-4 people. With multiple locations across Chicago and a casual, family-friendly atmosphere, it's a must-visit spot for anyone wanting to experience authentic Chicago-style pizza. Expect to pay around $30-40 for a large pizza.

Speciality: Deep-dish pizza

Hours: 10 AM - 10 PM

Price Range:. $15-25 per person

The Purple Pig

The Purple Pig is a celebrated Mediterranean restaurant on Michigan Avenue that's been a Chicago favorite since 2009. The menu focuses on bold, flavorful Mediterranean-inspired cuisine designed for sharing. They've earned a Michelin Bib Gourmand for exceptional quality and value. The atmosphere is lively and energetic, making it ideal for groups and casual dinners. They accept both walk-ins and reservations, with the bar area being particularly popular for spontaneous visits.

Speciality: Mediterranean cuisine

Hours: 11:30 AM - 12 PM daily

Price Range: $25-40 per person

Movies filmed in Illinois

Home Alone(1990)

The film Home Alone from 1990 was filmed in Illinois, specifically in the suburb of Winnetka, where the famous McCallister family home is located, but also in other locations in the Chicago area, such as O'Hare Airport.

View on IMDb

The Dark Knight(2008)

The Dark Knight from 2008 was filmed largely in Chicago, which was transformed into Gotham City. Iconic locations used included Navy Pier, LaSalle Street, and Lower Wacker Drive. Filming also included the old Chicago Post Office building.

View on IMDb

Child's Play(1988)

Child's Play form 1988 was filmed in Chicago, Illinois. Locations used included Cabrini Green, Brewster Apartments, and several streets on the South Side. The film used many real-life locations from the city to ensure authenticity.

Political System

The Illinois General Assembly is the state legislature, composed of the 118-member Illinois House of Representatives and the 59-member Illinois Senate. Representatives elect from their chamber a Speaker and Speaker pro tempore, and senators elect from the chamber a President of the Senate.

Famous People

Abraham Lincoln

Where he lived: Springfield, he lived there from 1837 until 1861, until he went to Washington as president.

Why is he famous: Abraham Lincoln was the 16th president of the United States and led the country during the Civil War, abolishing slavery with the Emancipation Proclamation.

Fun Fact: In his youth, Lincoln worked as a logger and river pilot before becoming a lawyer and politician.

Walt Disney

Where he lived: He was born in Chicago in 1901 and liveed there until 1906, when the family moved to a farm in Marceline, Missouri.

Why is he famous: Walt Disney was an American animator, film producer, voice actor, and entrepreneur. He was an innovator in the American animation industry and created the character Mickey Mouse in 1928.

Fun Fact: Disney's original choice of name was Mortimer Mouse, but his wife Lillian thought it was too pompous, and suggested Mickey instead.

Fun Facts about Illinois

The Windy City: Chicago isn't actually the windiest major US city - the nickname comes from politicians' "windy" speeches!

Skyscraper Birthplace: The world's first skyscraper was built in Chicago in 1885.

Reversed River: Chicago is the only city to permanently reverse a river's flow - the Chicago River now flows away from Lake Michigan.

Nuclear History: The first controlled nuclear reaction happened at University of Chicago in 1942.

Prairire State: Illinois once had 22 million acres of prairie - now less than 0.01% remains natural.
